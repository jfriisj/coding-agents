## Value Statement and Business Objective
As a Thesis Author, I want the thesis statement, report scaffold, and platform evidence baseline aligned into one active writing program, so that I can draft the master thesis without reopening scope or losing traceability to the implemented system.

## Objective
Establish the canonical baseline for the master thesis by verifying the alignment between the thesis statement, the LaTeX manuscript structure, and the existing implementation evidence, while explicitly enforcing the architectural invariants already identified for latency, scalability, robustness, evidence traceability, and privacy-safe evidence handling.

## Success Criteria
- Every thesis sub-question is mapped to a chapter destination and to an explicit architectural driver or design pattern.
- The evaluation narrative has an agreed privacy-safe Normal-vs-Debug telemetry baseline and a known evidence source for each performance or robustness claim.
- The shareable evidence set excludes credentials, raw debug traces, unsanitized identifiers, and unverified external artifacts before the drafting handoff is issued.
- The baseline mapping, utility tree, evidence ledger, and handoff package each have a named execution artifact destination under `agent-output/implementation/` that acts as the source of truth for Epic 1.1 outputs.
- Any claim lacking shareable, integrity-verified evidence is removed from the active `v0.1.0` claim set and recorded in the evidence ledger with a named later epic/release destination before handoff.
- The next thesis-writing handoff can proceed from a scope-locked baseline without re-deciding chapter ownership, architectural boundaries, or evidence intent.

## Assumptions
- The LaTeX files in `Analysis-and-design-of-a-platform-for-real-time-speech-translation-/report/` represent the current intended structure.
- The thesis statement in `case description/thesis_statement.tex` is the authoritative definition of the research questions.
- The existing benchmark, architecture, and privacy-safe logging artifacts under `Analysis-and-design-of-a-platform-for-real-time-speech-translation-/docs/` are the authoritative evidence inputs for this baseline phase.
- Existing evidence artifacts can be sanitized, integrity-checked, or explicitly deferred without expanding Epic 1.1 into implementation work.
- No new platform implementation work is required for this epic; it is a coordination, traceability, and scope-lock phase.

## Architectural and Security Constraints
- **Traceability Invariant**: Every research sub-question must map to a quality attribute or design pattern that can be defended in the Analysis and Design chapter.
- **Evidence Invariant**: Every planned latency, throughput, robustness, or quality claim must point to an existing evidence artifact or be explicitly deferred.
- **Boundary Invariant**: The manuscript must preserve the event-driven service-boundary narrative and avoid implying hidden synchronous coupling between pipeline stages.
- **Telemetry Baseline**: The plan must distinguish shareable Normal telemetry from non-shareable Debug telemetry before evaluation drafting begins.
- **Telemetry Sanitization Rule**: Shareable Normal telemetry must be limited to approved privacy-safe fields, sanitized correlation identifiers, and aggregate timing metrics; raw payloads, user-linked identifiers, and debug traces are excluded from thesis evidence.
- **Evidence Hygiene Rule**: Evidence inventory work must exclude or redact credentials, tokens, presigned URLs, and other sensitive operational details before an artifact is treated as shareable thesis evidence.
- **Integrity Rule**: External benchmark inputs, model artifacts, or generated evidence bundles referenced by the baseline must carry checksum or provenance verification, or else be marked non-authoritative and deferred.
- **Transport Security Requirement**: Boundary verification must explicitly confirm TLS or equivalent in-transit protection assumptions for distributed communication across ingress, orchestrator, broker, and model-service boundaries whenever the narrative implies cross-node deployment.
- **Scope-Lock Deferment Rule**: Any claim that lacks shareable, integrity-verified evidence at Epic 1.1 completion must be removed from the active `v0.1.0` claim set, recorded in the evidence ledger, and assigned to a named later roadmap epic/release before the handoff package is issued.

## Risks
- **Scope Creep**: Discovery of missing evidence during alignment could trigger unexpected implementation needs. *Mitigation: Record missing evidence in the evidence ledger, remove unsupported claims from the active `v0.1.0` claim set, and route them to a named later epic/release rather than expanding Epic 1.1 beyond baseline planning.*
- **Observability Gap**: Required per-hop telemetry may not be directly available in the current evidence set. *Mitigation: Lock the Normal-vs-Debug telemetry baseline and explicitly remove unsupported claims from the active scope unless they are deferred through the evidence ledger.*
- **Privacy Leakage**: Raw identifiers or debug traces could be pulled into thesis evidence if the shareable telemetry boundary is not enforced. *Mitigation: Require privacy-safe Normal telemetry fields only and exclude Debug artifacts from shareable evidence inventory.*
- **Evidence Contamination**: Existing operational runbooks may contain tokens or other sensitive data that are unsuitable for thesis use. *Mitigation: Run an evidence-sanitization pass and treat failing artifacts as non-shareable until redacted or excluded.*
- **Integrity Drift**: External evidence or generated bundles could be cited without provenance or checksum verification. *Mitigation: Record integrity verification status during source confirmation using the named existing proof sources and defer any unverified artifact from claim support.*
- **Boundary Drift**: The manuscript could accidentally describe a more synchronous architecture than the implemented event-driven system. *Mitigation: Verify service-boundary language against the system-architecture and architecture-findings artifacts before handoff.*
